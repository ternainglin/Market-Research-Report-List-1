<p><p>Bicycle share markets can be categorized into three main types. Point-to-point systems allow users to pick up bikes at one location and drop them off at another, promoting flexible travel between destinations. Ride-and-return models require users to return bikes to the original docking station, fostering a structured approach to bike use. Distributed markets operate without fixed stations, enabling users to park bikes anywhere within a designated area, enhancing accessibility and convenience while encouraging spontaneous usage.</p></p>
