<p><p>Cell separation technologies encompass various methods for isolating specific cell types from heterogeneous mixtures. Gradient centrifugation utilizes density gradients to separate cells based on size and density. Surface markers separation employs antibodies that target specific surface proteins for isolation. Fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS) sorts cells using fluorescent labeling and laser detection. Magnetic cell sorting involves attaching magnetic beads to target cells, allowing them to be separated via a magnetic field. Other methods include microfluidics and acoustic-based techniques, expanding the applications in research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
