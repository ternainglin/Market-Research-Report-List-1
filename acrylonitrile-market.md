The propylene method involves the catalytic reaction of propylene, ammonia, and air to produce acrylonitrile, offering higher yields and efficiency. In contrast, the propane method uses propane as a feedstock, resulting in a different production pathway that may have varied environmental impacts and cost dynamics. Both methods influence market pricing, demand, and the overall competitiveness of acrylonitrile in various applications.</p></p>
