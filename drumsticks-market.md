<p><p>The drumsticks market comprises three main types: drum brushes, beam rods, and mallets. Drum brushes are designed for soft, subtle sounds, often used in jazz or acoustic settings, featuring wire or nylon bristles. Beam rods, typically made of dowels, offer a unique sound that bridges the gap between brushes and traditional sticks, ideal for light drumming. Mallets, with softer heads, are suited for playing on softer surfaces like tympanis or xylophones, providing a warm, rounded tone. Each type caters to specific musical styles and preferences.</p></p>
