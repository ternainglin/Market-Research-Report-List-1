<p><p>The papermaking retention agent market includes various types, each enhancing fiber and filler retention during the papermaking process. Polyacrylamide (PAM) is widely used for its effective flocculating properties. Polyethyleneimine (PEI) serves as a cationic agent improving charge neutralization. Colloidal silica acts as a high-performance retention aid promoting fines and fillers stability. Bentonite, a natural clay, enhances water retention and improves paper quality. These agents collectively optimize paper production efficiency and influence the final product's characteristics.</p></p>
