<p><p>The residential Combined Heat and Power (CHP) market features three main technologies: fuel cells, engines, and microturbines. Fuel cells generate electricity through electrochemical reactions, offering high efficiency and low emissions. Engine-based systems, typically utilizing internal combustion engines, are popular for their reliability and lower upfront costs, though they may produce more emissions. Microturbines, compact and lightweight, convert fuel into electricity with lower emissions and noise, making them suitable for residential settings. Each technology caters to different consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
